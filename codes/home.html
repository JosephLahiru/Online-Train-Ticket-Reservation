Sri Devie Intercity Express Train

Sri Devie (ஶ்ரீதேவி) Intercity Express train runs daily between Colombo Fort to Kankesanthurai railway station and its service from 5th September, 2019. This Intercity train scheduled to begin its journey at 15:55 in the evening every day from Colombo Fort railway station and scheduled to reach the destination at 22:21 at the same day.

Its start journey on every morning at 03:45 from Kankesanthurai railway station and the express train will reach to Colombo Fort at 10:24 (in 6 hours and 39 min).

New Containers Cars and Oil Wagons for Sri Lanka Railways

Sri Lanka Railways received 25 tank wagons and 10 Container Cars from India. Sri Lanka Railways is planning to recommence rail transport of coal for Nuraichcholai Power Plant and Raw materials for Prima.

Power Cars for Aruwakkadu Waste Processing Facility (Metro Colombo Solid Waste Management Project)

Power cars specially made for Aruwakkadu Waste Processing Facility under Metro Colombo Solid Waste Management Project in Puttalam spotted while loading to a Vessel in China. Newly imported rolling stock (Class S14) for Sri Lanka Railway

New intercity express train named the "Denuwara Menike" will be deployed from Colombo Fort to Badulla on the upcountry line from First of November, 2019. The train is scheduled to begin its journey at 6.45 am in the morning every day from Colombo Fort and due to travel from Badulla to Fort on the following day. The express train will reach its destination in 8 hours and 42 min.

Transportation of 15000 tons of flour by the train per month

General Manager of Sri Lanka Railways, Mr. B.A.P. Ariyarathne stated that it is expected to transport 15000 tons of flour by the train per month in future. General Manager of Railways, futher stated that Department of Railways has signed an agreement on 27.06.2017 to transport stocks of preema flour from china harbour to Galle and Seeduwa and 15 wagons, transport containers have been granted to the Railway Department by the preema company under this project and it is due to earn a revenue of Rs 11 million per month for the Department of Sri Lanka Railway.
Mr B.A.P. Ariyarathne stated this project has been implemented under the objects of enhancing the condition of revenue of Railway Department, minimizing traffic congestion, occur due to the transportation of containers and minimizing the environmental pollution.
